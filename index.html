About me

Hello! I’m Nat Roschnik, a public-health nutritionist and researcher with more than 24 years of experience leading child health and nutrition programmes across 15+ countries for Save the Children. I currently serve as Senior Nutrition Advisor, helping teams design evidence-based programmes and innovative research to tackle malnutrition and improve early childhood development.

I’m passionate about harnessing technology—and now generative AI—to drive faster insights, stronger partnerships, and better outcomes for children worldwide.

Based in scenic Menthon-Saint-Bernard, France, I speak English, French, and a sprinkling of other languages, and I love learning new tools that spark creativity and impact.

About the course

Generative AI for Work & Research Productivity Bootcamp 2025 Q2 is an 8-week, hands-on programme that equips professionals with practical skills to:

  • Create polished websites, documents, and presentations with AI assistance
  • Perform literature reviews, analyse data, and build dashboards using intuitive AI tools
  • Design custom chatbots that answer domain-specific questions
  • Set up automated workflows that work around the clock
  • Create and edit images, videos, and design materials with AI
  • Develop real-world AI solutions that integrate into daily projects

By the end of the bootcamp, participants can harness AI to save time, boost quality, and solve complex challenges across research, creative industries, and business.
