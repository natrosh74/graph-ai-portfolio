About Me

I’m Nat Roschnik, a public health nutritionist with 24 years of experience supporting Save the Children’s programmes in nutrition, school health, malaria, WASH, HIV/AIDS, neglected tropical diseases, early childhood development, food security, and social protection across more than 15 countries. I’ve led and collaborated on nine cluster randomized trials and diverse research projects, bringing together partners, teams, and stakeholders to improve child nutrition and wellbeing. I’m passionate about using innovative tools, including generative AI, to enhance research, programme design, and real-world impact for children worldwide.

About the Course

Generative AI for Work & Research Productivity Bootcamp 2025 Q2 is an 8-week hands-on programme designed to help professionals learn to:

  • Create professional websites, documents, and presentations with AI
  • Perform literature reviews, analyze data, and build dashboards with intuitive AI tools
  • Design custom chatbots for domain-specific queries
  • Set up automated workflows
  • Utilize AI for creating and editing multimedia content
  • Develop practical AI solutions integrated into daily projects

By the end of this programme, I aim to harness AI to save time, improve quality, and solve real-world challenges across research, creative industries, and public health programming.
